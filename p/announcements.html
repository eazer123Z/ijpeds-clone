Policy on Multiple Submission (Simultaneous Submissions) and Prior Publication

Posted: 2025-11-06

Dear Authors,

The International Journal of Pediatrics is committed to upholding the highest standards of academic integrity and publishing ethics. Manuscripts must be original, unpublished, and not under consideration elsewhere.

Originality and Prior Publication: Work previously published in any form must be disclosed. Authors must ensure no substantial overlap with published work.

Simultaneous Submissions: Strictly prohibited. Can lead to copyright conflicts and duplication of review efforts.

Consequences: Formal Warning, Written Apology, Publication Ban (up to 1 year), Retraction.

Best regards,
IJPEDS Editorial Office

Should You Cite Yourself or Not? Avoid citing your published papers in this publisher whenever possible

Posted: 2025-02-15

Self-citation in academic publishing is a complex issue that can have a significant impact on how people perceive scholarly work. Institutional frameworks may unintentionally encourage authors to cite themselves, reducing the value of their work. Researchers should consider why authors self-cite and promote honesty in scholarly communication. Authors should exercise caution when citing their own work, particularly if they write for the same publisher. Over-reliance on one's own publications may unintentionally indicate a lack of interaction with the broader scholarly community.

Multiple Submission (Simultaneous Submissions) and Prior Publication

Posted: 2024-02-10

Multiple submission, or simultaneous submission, refers to the practice of submitting the same manuscript to more than one journal at the same time. This behavior is strictly prohibited as it violates ethical guidelines and journal policies.
