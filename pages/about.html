About the Journal

Focus and Scope

The International Journal of Pediatrics (IJPEDS) is the official publication of the Institute of Advanced Engineering and Science (IAES). The journal is open to submissions from scholars and experts in the wide areas of pediatric medicine and child health from all over the world.

The scope includes: General Pediatrics, Neonatology, Pediatric Surgery, Infectious Diseases, Vaccination, Nutrition and Growth, Developmental and Behavioral Pediatrics, Genetics and Rare Diseases, Child and Adolescent Mental Health, Emergency and Critical Care, and Global Child Health.

Peer Review Process

All submissions undergo double-blind peer review by at least two independent reviewers. The average review time is 4-6 weeks. The editorial team makes the final decision based on reviewer recommendations.

Open Access Policy

IJPEDS provides immediate open access to its content under the Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY) license.

Archiving

This journal utilizes the LOCKSS system to create a distributed archiving system among participating libraries.

Publication Ethics and Publication Malpractice Statement

IJPEDS adheres to the guidelines set by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE). All submitted manuscripts are screened for plagiarism. Authors must confirm originality and declare conflicts of interest.

IJPEDS Guide for Authors and Template

Authors should use the journal paper template. Title max 10 words. Abstract max 200 words, informative and self-explanatory. Structure: Introduction - Method - Results and Discussion - Conclusion. Min 10-25 references.

Checklist for Preparing Your Paper

  • Title: max 10 words, no acronym
  • Abstract: max 200 words, self-explanatory
  • Keywords: 3-6 relevant terms
  • References: min 10-25, primarily journal articles
  • Figures: numbered, captioned below, cited in text
  • Tables: numbered, captioned above, single horizontal lines

Policy of Plagiarism Detection

All manuscripts are screened using automated plagiarism detection tools. Manuscripts with similarity index above 20% will be returned to authors.

Withdrawal of Manuscripts

Authors may withdraw manuscripts before the review process begins. Withdrawal after review has started requires a formal written request to the editorial office.
